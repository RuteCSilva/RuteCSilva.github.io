Reading Summary

Unit 1

Crawford, S. L. (2006) Correlation and Regression. Circulation 114(9): 2083–2088. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.105.586495

Crawford (2006) introduces the concepts of correlation and regression in statistical analysis. Correlation is a measure of the strength and direction of the relationship between two variables, while regression analysis is a statistical method used to examine the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. The author explains the different types of correlation and regression, including linear, curvilinear, and multiple regression, and provides examples of how these methods can be applied in medical research. The article also discusses the importance of understanding and interpreting correlation and regression coefficients, and how to assess the statistical significance of these relationships. Overall, the article provides a useful overview of correlation and regression analysis for readers who may be less familiar with these concepts.

Zhao, X. & Li, C. (2018) 'Deep Learning in Social Computing', In: Deng, L., Liu, Y. (eds) Deep Learning in Natural Language Processing. Singapore: Springer. 255-288. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-981-10-5209-5_9

Zhao and Li (2018) explore the applications of deep learning in social computing, which is a field that studies the intersection of social behaviour and computational systems. The authors provide an overview of deep learning techniques, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs), recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and deep belief networks (DBNs), and discuss how these methods can be used in social computing applications, such as sentiment analysis, opinion mining, and recommender systems. The article also discusses the challenges and limitations of deep learning in social computing, such as the need for large amounts of labelled data and the potential for bias and ethical concerns. Overall, the article provides a comprehensive overview of the potential and challenges of applying deep learning in social computing research.

Unit 7

Sharma, A. (2017) Understanding Activation Functions in Neural Networks. Available at: https://medium.com/the-theory-of-everything/understanding-activation-functions-in-neural-networks-9491262884e0 [Accessed on 01/04/2023]

Sharma (2017) provides an overview of activation functions in neural networks, which are mathematical functions that are applied to the output of a neuron to introduce non-linearity into the model. The author explains the role of activation functions in allowing neural networks to learn complex patterns and relationships in data, and describes several common types of activation functions, such as sigmoid, ReLU, and tanh. The article also discusses the advantages and disadvantages of different activation functions, and how to choose the appropriate one for a particular problem. Overall, the article provides a helpful introduction to the importance and use of activation functions in neural networks.

Vaio, A.D., Palladino, R., Hassan, R. & Escobar, O. (2020) Artificial intelligence and business models in the sustainable development goals perspective: A systematic literature review. Journal of Business Research 121: 283-314. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbusres.2020.08.019

Vaio et al. (2020) conducted a systematic literature review to explore the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI), business models, and sustainable development goals (SDGs). They found that AI can be leveraged to enable sustainable business models that promote the SDGs, such as reducing waste and greenhouse gas emissions. However, the authors also noted that AI can exacerbate existing inequalities and contribute to unethical business practices if not properly regulated. They suggest that policymakers and businesses should prioritize a human-centric approach to AI development and implementation, while also considering the potential impacts on the environment and society.
The study conducted by Vaio et al. (2020) did not specifically focus on artificial neural networks (ANNs), but rather on the broader concept of artificial intelligence (AI). ANNs are a type of machine learning algorithm that is used in AI, but the authors did not delve into the technical details of different AI techniques or algorithms. Instead, they examined the role of AI more broadly in enabling sustainable business models that promote the SDGs. However, it is worth noting that ANNs can be used in various applications related to sustainability, such as predicting energy consumption or optimizing resource allocation, which can contribute to the achievement of the SDGs.

Unit 8

Mach, P. (2021) 10 Business Applications of Neural Network.

In "10 Business Applications of Neural Network", Mach (2021) provides an overview of the potential uses of neural networks in various business applications. The author describes ten different scenarios where neural networks can be used to solve business problems, such as predicting customer churn, optimizing supply chain management, and detecting fraud. Each scenario is accompanied by a description of the neural network model that could be used, as well as examples of real-world applications and benefits. The article also discusses the advantages and limitations of neural networks in business, such as the need for large amounts of data and computational power. Overall, the article serves as a useful resource for readers who are interested in the potential applications of neural networks in business settings.

CDEI (2019) Snapshot Paper - AI and Personal Insurance.

The Centre for Data Ethics and Innovation (CDEI) released a Snapshot Paper in 2019 on the topic of AI and Personal Insurance. The paper provides an overview of how artificial intelligence (AI) is being used in the insurance industry to improve the accuracy and efficiency of risk assessment, underwriting, and claims management. The paper discusses the potential benefits of AI in insurance, such as better pricing and greater accessibility to insurance products, as well as the challenges and risks associated with the use of AI, such as the potential for bias and discrimination. The paper also highlights some of the ethical considerations and policy implications of AI in insurance, such as the need for transparency and accountability in decision-making, and the importance of ensuring that AI systems are used in ways that are fair and ethical. Overall, the Snapshot Paper provides a useful overview of the current state of AI in personal insurance and the key issues that need to be addressed to ensure that its use is responsible and beneficial.

Hutson, M. (2021) Robo-writers: the rise and risks of language-generating AI.

In "Robo-writers: the rise and risks of language-generating AI", Hutson (2021) discusses the increasing use of language-generating AI in various fields, such as journalism, advertising, and social media. The article explores the potential benefits of this technology, such as the ability to produce content quickly and efficiently, as well as the risks and challenges associated with it. These include concerns about the quality and authenticity of the generated content, as well as the potential for misuse and abuse, such as the spread of disinformation and propaganda. The article also highlights some of the ethical and legal considerations that need to be addressed in the development and deployment of language-generating AI, such as issues of intellectual property and responsibility for the content produced by these systems. Overall, the article provides a thought-provoking analysis of the current state of language-generating AI and the complex ethical and societal issues that it raises.

Castelvecchi, D. (2016) Can we open the black box of AI?

“Can we open the black box of AI?" by Castelvecchi (2016) is an article that discusses the "black box" problem in artificial intelligence (AI). The "black box" refers to the internal workings of AI algorithms that are often difficult to interpret or understand. The article explores the implications of this problem, such as the potential for bias and the difficulty of assessing the reliability and safety of AI systems. The article discusses various approaches to opening the black box of AI, such as using explainable AI techniques that provide a clearer understanding of how the system makes decisions and developing new tools and frameworks for auditing and testing AI systems. The article also highlights some of the challenges and limitations of these approaches, such as the trade-off between explainability and accuracy in AI systems, and the difficulty of developing standardized methods for evaluating AI systems. Overall, the article provides a thought-provoking analysis of the "black box" problem in AI and the ongoing efforts to address this important issue.

Mayo, M. (2017) Explained: Updating Weights with Gradient Descent & Backpropagation.

In "Explained: Updating Weights with Gradient Descent & Backpropagation", Mayo (2017) provides an overview of the gradient descent algorithm and backpropagation, which are two key techniques used in training artificial neural networks. The article explains how the gradient descent algorithm is used to minimize the error in a neural network by adjusting the weights of the connections between the neurons. Backpropagation is then introduced as a method for efficiently calculating the gradient of the error function with respect to the weights. The article also discusses different variants of gradient descent, such as stochastic gradient descent and batch gradient descent, and how they can affect the speed and accuracy of training. Finally, the article provides a step-by-step explanation of how the gradient descent algorithm and backpropagation are used in practice to train a simple neural network. Overall, the article provides a clear and accessible introduction to these important concepts in neural network training.

Unit 9

Wall, M. (2019) Biased and wrong? Facial recognition tech in the dock.

In "Biased and wrong? Facial recognition tech in the dock", Wall (2019) discusses the controversy surrounding the use of facial recognition technology by law enforcement agencies and the potential for bias and error in the system. The article highlights the lack of transparency and oversight in the development and deployment of facial recognition technology, as well as the limited testing and evaluation of the accuracy and reliability of the technology. The article also discusses some of the ethical and legal issues associated with the use of facial recognition technology, such as privacy concerns and the potential for discrimination and abuse. The article concludes by calling for greater regulation and transparency in the development and use of facial recognition technology to ensure that it is used in a fair and responsible manner. Overall, the article provides a critical analysis of the potential risks and challenges associated with the use of facial recognition technology and the need for greater accountability and oversight in its development and deployment.

Parisi, L., Neagu, D., Ma, R. & Campean, F. (2022) Quantum ReLU activation for Convolutional Neural Networks to improve diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease and COVID-19. Elsevier Ltd. DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2021.115892

In "Quantum ReLU activation for Convolutional Neural Networks to improve diagnosis of Parkinson's disease and COVID-19", Parisi et al. (2022) propose the use of quantum ReLU activation for convolutional neural networks to improve the accuracy of diagnosing Parkinson's disease and COVID-19. The article describes how the quantum ReLU activation function can be used to improve the performance of the convolutional neural network by reducing the effect of vanishing gradients and improving the accuracy of the model. The authors also discuss how the proposed method can be applied to medical diagnosis, particularly for the detection of Parkinson's disease and COVID-19, which can be challenging due to the complex and variable nature of the symptoms. The article provides experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the accuracy of diagnosis for these diseases. Overall, the article highlights the potential of quantum ReLU activation for improving the performance of convolutional neural networks in medical diagnosis, particularly for complex and challenging conditions such as Parkinson's disease and COVID-19.

Wang, J., Turko, R., Shaikh, O., Park, H., Das, N., Hohman, F., Kahng, M. & Chau, P. (2021) CNN Explainer: Learn Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in your browser.

In "CNN Explainer: Learn Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in your browser", Wang et al. (2021) present a web-based tool for visualizing and understanding the workings of convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The article describes how the tool, called CNN Explainer, allows users to interact with and explore the various layers and operations of a CNN, providing an intuitive and accessible way to learn about the inner workings of these complex models. The authors discuss the design and development of the tool, highlighting its features and functionality, and provide examples of how it can be used to gain insight into the behavior of different types of CNNs. The article concludes by emphasizing the potential of CNN Explainer for enhancing the transparency and interpretability of CNNs and for promoting greater understanding and engagement with this important technology. Overall, the article presents an innovative and useful tool for visualizing and understanding CNNs, which has the potential to improve the accessibility and effectiveness of machine learning education and research.

Novaković, J.Dj., Veljović, A., Ilić, S.S., Papić, Z. & Milica, T. (2017) Evaluation of Classification Models in Machine Learning. Theory and Applications of Mathematics & Computer Science 7(1): 39-46.

Novaković et al. (2017) present an overview of classification models in machine learning and discuss the importance of evaluating their performance. The article outlines the basic concepts and methods of classification, including decision trees, k-nearest neighbours, and support vector machines. The authors then discuss the metrics commonly used to evaluate classification models, such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, and highlight their strengths and limitations. The article also covers the issue of overfitting and the importance of cross-validation in evaluating the performance of classification models. Finally, the authors provide a practical example of evaluating the performance of a classification model on a real-world dataset. Overall, the article provides a useful overview of the key concepts and methods of classification in machine learning and highlights the importance of rigorous evaluation and testing of classification models to ensure their effectiveness and reliability in real-world applications.

Unit 11

Joprdan, J. (2017) Evaluating a machine learning model.

Jordan (2017) provides a comprehensive guide to evaluating a machine learning model. The article covers the basics of model evaluation, including the importance of choosing an appropriate evaluation metric based on the specific problem and data, and the need to use a separate validation set to test the model's performance. The author also discusses the issue of overfitting, and presents several techniques to address this problem, such as regularization, cross-validation, and early stopping. Additionally, the article covers the importance of understanding the trade-offs between different evaluation metrics, and how to interpret the results of model evaluation in the context of the specific problem and data. Finally, the author highlights some common pitfalls to avoid when evaluating machine learning models, such as relying too much on a single evaluation metric and failing to consider the broader implications and limitations of the model. Overall, the article provides a valuable guide for practitioners and researchers in the field of machine learning and highlights the importance of rigorous and thoughtful evaluation of machine learning models.

Nighania, K. (2018) Various ways to evaluate a machine learning model’s performance.

Nighania (2018) discusses various ways to evaluate the performance of machine learning models. The article starts by emphasizing the importance of evaluating models using a separate validation set, rather than the training set, to avoid overfitting. The author then presents several commonly used evaluation metrics, such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score, ROC curve, and AUC score, and explains the advantages and limitations of each. The article also covers techniques for comparing the performance of multiple models, such as cross-validation and statistical tests. Additionally, the author highlights the importance of considering the context and objectives of the specific problem when selecting an appropriate evaluation metric, and the need to interpret the results of model evaluation in a meaningful and actionable way. Finally, the article provides some practical tips for evaluating machine learning models, such as using visualization tools to gain insights into the model's behaviour and setting up a rigorous experimental protocol to ensure reproducibility. Overall, the article provides a useful overview of the key considerations and techniques for evaluating machine learning models.

Unit 12

CDEI (2019) Deepfakes and Audiovisual Disinformation.

The Centre for Data Ethics and Innovation (CDEI) published a report in 2019 addressing the issue of deepfakes and audio-visual disinformation. Deepfakes refer to manipulated videos that are created using artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) algorithms. Such videos can be used to deceive, misinform, or defame individuals or entities. The report highlights the potential risks associated with deepfakes and the challenges in addressing them. It also examines the different types of deepfakes and the technological advancements that make their creation easier. The report presents several recommendations for policymakers, social media platforms, and the technology industry to combat deepfakes, including developing clear policies, improving AI detection methods, and educating the public about the existence and impact of deepfakes.
